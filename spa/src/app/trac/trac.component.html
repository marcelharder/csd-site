Trac for Hospitals

Motivation:
This program is a data-entry application which was first developed in the 1980's in The
Netherlands. TRAC went through many incarnations and is now a web application. The
motivation for development of TRAC was (and still is) to streamline the administrative
side of cardiac surgery. As a surgeon doing essentially the same operation over and
over again we did not like to dictate similar operative reports everyday. And we
thought that enlisting the help of the rest of the operative team would be a clever
solution to our "problem". The objective was to have the operative report ready before
the patient even left the operative theatre and with minimal involvement of the
surgeon. We would tell the nurse which distal anastomosis we were doing and she
would put that information in the program. Before the skin was closed the report was
generated by the program and printed in the operative theatre. After the succesful
conclusion of our first mission with the operative reports we extended TRAC to include
the discharge summary, another boring excercise for us. Now TRAC also generates a
discharge summary at the end of the admission of the patient.
Next page ->
Round about 2002 we decided to include measurement of surgical outcomes in TRAC.
We realized that we were collecting valuable data with TRAC and we liked to do
something serious with it. In order to reach this goal we changed the underlying
database from our homegrown "minimal dataset" to the dataset required for the
European Cardiac Surgery Registry. Now we were able to calculate our surgical
outcomes, because we used the EuroScore for pre-operative risk assesment and the
database for the actual surgical outcome. We used the Variable Life Adjusted Display
method (VLAD's) to graphically display our outcomes, both for individual surgeons and
for the institution as a whole.
